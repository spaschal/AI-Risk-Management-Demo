What is the purpose of the AI Governance Hub?

The AI Governance Hub exists to guide and support responsible use of AI across the company. It ensures that AI solutions are developed and deployed in alignment with enterprise standards for ethics, risk, compliance, and operational excellence. Whether you're exploring a new AI tool or preparing for production deployment, the Hub provides the resources and connections you need to do it safely and effectively.

The Hub is a centralized internal site that offers:

  • Governance templates and standards
  • Risk assessment tools
  • AI project dashboards
  • Guidance for trials, POCs, and production deployment
  • Access to the AI Governance team and review board
Does reviewing the AI Governance Hub automatically ensure compliance?

The AI Governance Hub provides tools, templates, and guidance to help you stay compliant, but compliance is not automatic. You must actively participate by completing intake and risk assessment forms, using provided templates and checklists, following the governance review process, and implementing required controls and monitoring.

What is the difference between AI Center of Excellence and AI Governance Hub?

AI Center of Excellence (AI CoE): Focuses on innovation, enablement, and scaling of AI across the organization. It provides strategic direction, technical expertise, and promotes best practices for AI development and adoption.

AI Governance Hub: Focuses on risk management, compliance, and oversight. It ensures AI solutions meet ethical, legal, and operational standards before they are deployed or scaled.

In short, the AI CoE helps you build and grow AI, while the AI Governance Hub helps you do it responsibly and safely.

What is AI Governance?

AI Governance is the structured oversight of AI systems to ensure they are safe, ethical, compliant, and effective. It includes risk assessments, policy alignment, and monitoring to prevent unintended consequences and ensure responsible innovation. By establishing clear guidelines and accountability, AI Governance & Enablement helps organizations build trust and maintain control over their AI initiatives.

What is Responsible AI?

Responsible AI means developing and deploying artificial intelligence systems in ways that are ethical, transparent, and aligned with both organizational values and societal norms. Key principles include promoting fairness, safeguarding privacy, ensuring reliability and safety, providing transparency in decision-making, maintaining human oversight, and complying with laws and ethical standards.

How do I stay compliant with AI Governance requirements?

Staying compliant requires active participation and documentation.

  1. Use the provided guidance in the AI Risk Assessment Standard located within the Hub
  2. Complete the AI Governance Review Request Form for each project
  3. Follow the governance review process before production deployment
  4. Implement required controls and monitoring (logging, role-based access, incident response)
  5. Engage with the AI Governance team for guidance and support

Compliance is not automatic—you must engage with the process and maintain documentation. If you do not follow the governance process, you may experience project delays until required controls are in place, denial of governance review approval, or escalation to senior leadership. For additional assistance, email AIGovernance@parivedasolutions.com.

How does Responsible AI promote fairness and transparency?

Responsible AI promotes fairness by minimizing bias and discrimination while supporting transparency to make decision-making processes clear and understandable. It also ensures privacy and data protection, reliable operation under diverse conditions, and maintains accountability through human oversight.

To ensure Responsible AI during deployment, organizations should:

  • Conduct risk assessments and complete intake forms
  • Configure logging and monitoring
  • Apply role-based access controls
  • Document adversarial testing results
  • Establish incident response plans
  • Track reporting and mitigation actions

These practices help maintain ethical standards, operational safety, and compliance throughout the AI lifecycle.

What is adversarial testing for AI solutions?

Adversarial testing is a process used to evaluate how well an AI system can handle tricky or unexpected inputs. It involves presenting the AI with confusing, misleading, or intentionally crafted data to see if it makes mistakes. Additionally, it improves AI reliability by exposing weaknesses in the model, allowing teams to address potential flaws before deployment. By challenging the AI with edge cases and unexpected scenarios, it ensures the system behaves correctly under diverse conditions, enhances security, and reduces the risk of errors in real-world use.

Examples of adversarial testing in AI include:

  • Modifying images so they look the same to humans but trick the AI into misclassification
  • Altering text inputs to confuse natural language models
  • Injecting noise or unexpected patterns into data to test model resilience

These techniques help identify and fix vulnerabilities, making AI solutions more reliable and secure.

What is the AI Inventory?

The AI Inventory is a centralized list of Generative AI tools that have been formally reviewed by the AI Governance Team. It helps employees understand which tools are approved/not approved, what risks were evaluated, and the specific approval requirements for using them across the company (including legacy business units).

Always check the AI Inventory before introducing or requesting any new AI tool or vendor. It prevents duplicate capabilities, unnecessary costs, and unvetted tools entering the company systems. If you unsure, please contact AI Governance.

How often is the AI Inventory reviewed or updated?

The AI Governance Team conducts quarterly reviews of all approved tools to ensure:

  • Ongoing safety and risk compliance
  • Continued cost-effectiveness and business value
  • Alignment with the company policies and Responsible AI standards
  • Usage remains consistent with the approved purpose and scope
How do I request a new AI tool?

Before submitting your request complete the following:

  1. Review the AI Governance Hub (oversight expectations, risk processes, and standards).
  2. Start the vendor onboarding process through Supply Chain (procurement + contracting).
  3. Complete the IT Security Vendor Assessment; this assessment is required and must be completed prior to submission.
  4. Confirm that the tool is not already approved by reviewing the AI Inventory.

Once confirmed, submit an AI Tool Request Form.

What happens after I submit a formal AI tool request?

The AI Governance Team evaluates the tool along several dimensions, including:

  • AI Capabilities Risk
  • Responsible AI risks (safety, transparency, fairness, compliance)
  • Security & Privacy alignment

After evaluation, the AI Governance team will notify you of the decision. The notification will include the approval status, any required usage conditions, and clear next steps, if applicable.

What if another department already uses a similar tool?

AI Governance will conduct a feature overlap assessment as part of the evaluation process. If an existing approved tool provides equivalent or better functionality, the new request may be declined to avoid unnecessary duplication and costs.

How long does the evaluation process take?

Evaluation timelines vary based on several factors, including:

  • Completion of the Vendor Assessment
  • Complexity and risk profile of the tool
  • Availability and quality of security, privacy, and compliance documentation
  • Number of approvers needed for the review

The AI Governance team cannot begin its review until the Vendor Assessment is completed in full.

What's the difference between Supply Chain approval and an AI Governance review?

Supply Chain remains responsible for vendor onboarding, contracting, data agreements, and security approvals in accordance with the company's standard processes.

AI Governance evaluates AI-specific capabilities, risks, and responsible-use requirements.

These processes are complementary and operate in parallel—together ensuring AI tools are both responsibly governed and formally approved for enterprise use.

Why is the IT Security Vendor Assessment required first?

The IT Security Vendor Assessment establishes the vendor's security baseline, which determines whether AI Governance can safely and effectively evaluate AI-specific risks. Skipping this step may create gaps in risk visibility and lead to downstream delays. As a result, completion of the IT Security Vendor Assessment is a required prerequisite for all AI tool requests.

Can I request a tool through Microsoft Teams chat or direct messaging?

AI tool requests must go through the formal process. Requests submitted via Teams or direct messaging are at risk of being missed or lost, resulting in incomplete documentation.
